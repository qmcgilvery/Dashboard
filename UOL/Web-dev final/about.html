What we do

Mo-go is all about developing and testing your capacity to perform work. We incorporate a variety of modalities to achieve a balanced, high and safe work load to get the most out of each person. Indoor rowing, cycling and cross country skiing, all on Concept 2 machines, provide a safe way to train aerobic, lactate threshold and anaerobic capacities. We use these, along with running, as the primary tools to increase overall fitness. They are the cornerstones of our conditioning programs for a variety of reasons

  • They are the best at eliciting a metabolic response
  • They operate synergistically and complementarily
  • Rowing is a near total body way to train muscular endurance and aerobic capacity
  • Cycling is also way to train muscular endurance and aerobic capacity but with a primary emphasis on the lower body
  • Skiing on the Concept 2 is a primarily upper body activity that can be used for both aerobic and anaerobic conditioning
  • Running is often the best way for people to achieve their highest possible output in terms of V02 max and heart rate
  • They are the safest way to challenge you to the maximum
  • By implementing multiple modalities we can reduce the risk of overuse injuries common in endurance activities and provide a more well rounded fitness.

We also employ weightlifting and bodyweight resistance exercises to build strength and enhance mobility. The improvement of strength and mobility is designed to have carry over to the other training modalities. The goal is to develop your fitness base as wide as possible.
